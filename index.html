Historical background

Relations between the UK and the rest of Europe have always been a complicated affair. Before getting into the study, below is a summary of the major points of the UK-EU relation:

  • 1950

    The United Kingdom refuses to participate in the ECSC

    Robert Schuman, one of the founding fathers of Europe, presents his plan for the creation of a European Coal and Steel Community (ECSC). The United Kingdom refuses to participate.

  • 1963

    France opposes the integration of the UK into the EEC

    Created in 1957, the European Economic Community (EEC) continues the work undertaken by the ECSC. In 1963 and 1967, France rejected the two British proposals to join the EEC.

  • 1973

    UK's entry into the EEC

    The United Kingdom's third application to join the EEC is accepted. Consulted by referendum, the British approved the integration.

  • 1979

    Margaret Thatcher "wants her money back"

    In 1979, the then elected Prime Minister, Margaret Thatcher, entered into conflict with the European Union and demanded a reduction of her country's contribution to the Community budget.

  • 1992

    UK ratifies Maastricht Treaty but rejects single currency

    Conservative John Major ratifies the Maastricht Treaty, but obtains a clause allowing the UK to keep their national currency.

  • 2016

    The British vote to leave the EU

    On the 23rd of June 2016, the British voted to leave the European Union by 52% to 48%.

  • 2020

    The United Kingdom leaves the European Union

    After more than three and a half years of negotiations, two general elections, and three Prime Ministers, the Brexit is finally effective at midnight on 31 January.
